Some notes on the Quran

The structure of the Quran

The Muslims consider the Quran as a continuation of the scriptures that came before it, the Torah, Psalms, books sent to Moses and Jesus. We also believe that the Quran is the final and last Testament to mankind.

Unlike a conventional book divided into chapters one following the other, the chapters in the Quran are each a theme, they are connected but arecomplete and unique within itself. The names does not necessary indicate the contents of this theme. Some chapters like say, Suratul Maryam is the story about Mary (pbuh), the mother of Jesus (pbuh) and also includes the story of Abraham (pbuh) and other prophets (pbut) when it relates to the theme of that chapter. The story of Mary (pbuh) is also in other chapters where it relates to the theme of that chapter.

The Quran begins with the Fatihah, opening chapter, the short chapter of seven verses that captures the relationship of humans and Allah,(God).

The second Chapter begins with the words to the seekers of truth. It describes who will benefit and gain the guidance of the Quran and those who will not. The rest of the chapter, contains many stories about prophets found in the bible. It's important to remember that the Quran was revealed to the prophet (pbuh) by the angel Gabriel over a period of 23 years. The existing Quran is not arranged in chronological order as it was revealed. The final order was revealed to the prophet (pbuh) and he was told the order that the chapters and verses should be arranged. There are one hundred and fourteen chapters in the Quran.

The Quran can be roughly divided into two types of revelation, the revelations that came to the prophet in Mecca, the early years of his prophethood, and the revelations that came in Medina called Meccan and Medinite surahs respectively.

The significance of this division is, Meccan Surahs teach the people about the nature of Allah. That he is one, and not many. These early teachings are about the existence of Allah and the nature of his relationship with his creation and the ethics that deal with our relations to each other. This is known as Aqeedah, correct belief.

The Medinite surahs were revealed after the prophets migration to Medina, where he began to establish a religion and a state. This part of the Quran contains the rules and regulations and the dealings with our neighbours and other states. As at this time he was in conflict with the idolators, the rules of war were also revealed to the prophet (pbuh).

So when learning about Islam it's important to first gain correct understanding of the nature of Allah. Once having understood the nature of Allah you can never despair of the mercy of Allah.

Only then are the laws and rituals taught, to understand what Allah expects of us to lead a good life.

Guidance for dealing with the realities of life on a daily basis

The reality of our existence

The reality of our existence is we are born, we live our life span and then we die. This reality is important to remember everyday. What tends to happen with all of us is although we know this to be true, we park this realisation in a place at the back of our minds and sometimes we act as if we have all of eternity to do the things we want to do.

Allah has gifted us with mechanisms to help us to survive. When the body needs replenishment we feel hungry. When we fear a danger adrenaline is pumped into the body to help us to cope. Every single mechanisms to survive we have been gifted with.

What is the equivalent gift for the soul. The soul reaches for purity and when it touches purity it comforted. It continues to to reach out, and the default of mankind is to be good.

The other reality is whether we accept it or not all indications from every religion shows that a divine hand controls our lives. We have but only limited self-will to control the affairs in our lives. This world is only a fleeting instance in the eternity of time.

Worship is for our benefit

Allah does not need our worship, we need Allahs guidance in big things and in small things. Once you believe in Allah, and agree to obey his commands your life changes.

It does not mean that you will not be tested, life is created with struggle as this struggle is necessary for progress. With belief your attitude to life changes. Whatever happens to you, you know it is Allahs will and you put your trust in him. He has promised that he is your protector. Your frame of reference is not a lifetime, its an eternity. You desire the good of this world and the hereafter. Any discomfort even a pin prick will be compensated. You are grateful for everything. The common Islamic expression is "Alhamdulila" it's connotation is gratefulness for the blessing mixed with proclaiming praise to the giver of the blessing. There is scientific evidence to prove that everybody's happiness depends on the level of gratitude in their life. You can read about it here.

The reality of the prophets existence

When you study the life of the prophet (pbuh), you will realise that his test was far harder than anything we will ever go through. Some of these tests and trials he faced like the persecution in Mecca, the disastrous visit to Taif where he was stoned and chased away by the people. Yet his faith in Allah was unwavering and his focus on his mission fixed. Not all his prayers were immediately answered. In some cases it was delayed and sometimes it was not granted, yet he accepted Allah's will and his faith never wavered.

Our relationship to God

We are asked to seek Allahs help with prayer and with patience as Allah is always with those who are patient. We have no one else worth worshipping and only he is our helper.

The positive approach to Islam is to see how actions earn rewards. Not to look at how sins earn punishments. We were created weak and so our sincere repentance is accepted by the most forgiving and most merciful.

For example in the Quran we are asked to "... Eat and drink, but waste not ..". We are rewarded every time you eat and drink without wasting food. How easy is that to earn rewards..

The sin Allah does not like is associating partners with him. He forgives those who repent because he is the most forgiving the most merciful.

We can guide our lives by retrospection. We acknowledge what we do wrong. There are no intermediaries between Allah and the believer. Allah is the most merciful, most compassionate and most forgivng. He says his mercy exceeds his wrath. No one needs despair of his forgiveness.
